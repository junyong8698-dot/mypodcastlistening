### The Significance of FNG at 10

A Fear & Greed reading of 10 deserves its own discussion. In OWUW Beat's research (back when R&D was still alive), we established that **FNG is a lagging indicator** — BTC price drives FNG, not the other way around. Sentiment follows price with a delay.

However, we also found that extreme readings have predictive power at the margins:
- **Extreme Greed (FNG > 90)**: Not contrarian — it's momentum. BTC returned +32% on average in the following month with 85% win rate.
- **Extreme Fear (FNG < 20)**: Mean reversion signal. "Strong sell" readings historically preceded buying opportunities — 75% win rate for contrarian longs.

An FNG of 10 is *deep* in the contrarian buy zone by historical standards. But our systematic model doesn't trade sentiment — it trades regime. And the regime says BEAR. This is where human traders get whipsawed: "But the fear index says buy!" The model doesn't care about the fear index. The model cares about the 200-day moving average, dollar strength, copper-gold ratios, and net liquidity. All of which still say: wait.

## Interesting Moments

### 1. FNG at 10: Is This the Bottom?

The Fear & Greed Index hitting 10 is remarkable. This is a market where:
- BTC is only 3% above where it was 30 days ago
- The price has actually *recovered* from its deepest lows
- Yet fear is at its most extreme reading

This divergence — price recovering while fear intensifies — usually means one of two things:
- Smart money is quietly accumulating while retail panic sells (bullish)
- The "bounce" was a dead cat and the real pain is still coming (bearish)

Our model doesn't try to resolve this ambiguity. It measures regime factors and waits. But as a human writing this blog, I'll note: FNG at 10 is the kind of reading that shows up in "best time to buy" retrospectives. Whether *this specific instance* will be one of those is unknowable in real time.

### 2. The 50-Day MA Knife Edge

With BTC dropping below $70K, we may have lost the one bullish factor in our regime model. The 50-day MA sits around $71K, and BTC at $69,330 is clearly below it. If this holds through the next assessment window, the regime score drops from -4 to -5 — the most bearish reading possible.

This is interesting because it means the recovery from March 9 ($66,961) to March 17 (~$74,262) was enough to briefly reclaim the 50-day MA, but the last few days have given it all back. Classic bull trap in a bear market.

### 3. The Stablecoin Paradox

OWUW now has exposure to two competing stablecoins:
- **OWUW Beat**: Holds $68.73 in **USDT** (Tether) via Binance Flexible Earn
- **BlackJyong**: Owns 2 shares of **CRCL** (Circle), the company behind **USDC**

The CEO is simultaneously using Tether's product and investing in Tether's competitor. This isn't necessarily contradictory — USDT is the better stablecoin for trading (more liquid, more pairs on Binance), while USDC/Circle is the better equity investment (regulated, transparent, growth story). But it's a fun quirk of a multi-strategy operation.

### 4. The Discipline Tax

Thirty-five days of BEAR regime with 0% allocation. BTC has been as low as $66,961 and as high as $74,262 during this period. A human trader — especially one watching FNG at 10 — would be sorely tempted to "just buy a little." The model says no. The model has been right about avoiding the downside, even if it missed the short-term bounce.

This is what we call the "discipline tax" — the psychological cost of trusting a system when your gut says otherwise. It's free in dollar terms but expensive in emotional terms. And it's the single biggest reason most people can't do systematic trading: not because the models don't work, but because the models demand a kind of patience that feels unnatural.
