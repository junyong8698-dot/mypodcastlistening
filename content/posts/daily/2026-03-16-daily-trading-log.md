## R&D Research: The State of the Art

### What We Know Works (V5.3 Core Principles)

After weeks of intensive R&D, certain truths have crystallized about BTC trading strategies:

1. **Regime detection beats signal timing.** Knowing *whether* to be in the market matters more than knowing *when* to enter. V5.3's six-factor regime model captures this insight.

2. **Crash defense overlays are the secret sauce.** V5.3's three overlays — crash threshold (-5% in 3 days), macro urgency (regime score 5-day rate of change < -2 triggers 30% reduction), and bear vol-targeting (30-day vol > 30% triggers position reduction) — are what separate it from simpler regime models.

3. **BTC is structurally bullish long-term.** Short selling, daily-rebalanced leverage, and pure macro timing all lose to buy-and-hold over full cycles. The only winning strategy is asymmetric: be long when conditions are favorable, be flat when they're not.

4. **The Fear & Greed Index lags price, not the other way around.** FNG > 90 (Extreme Greed) actually signals momentum continuation (+32% in 1 month, 85% win rate), not reversal. Conversely, today's Extreme Fear reading at 23 tells us what the *crowd* thinks, not where price is going.

5. **"Strong sell" signals are buying opportunities.** Signals that look most bearish (mean reversion setups) have a 75% win rate. The crowd is usually wrong at extremes.

### What Doesn't Work (The Graveyard)

The R&D graveyard is growing. Strategies that have been tested and rejected:

- **Machine learning models** (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting): ~52% accuracy with ~300 weekly samples. Not enough data for ML to work on BTC.
- **Short selling**: Structural long-term loser on a structurally bullish asset.
- **Daily-rebalanced leverage**: Volatility decay destroys returns. 3x daily BTC would have lost money in the last month even as BTC went up.
- **Kelly criterion sizing**: Mathematically optimal, psychologically devastating. Full Kelly on BTC gives you +94,000% returns with -65% drawdowns. Half Kelly is still too aggressive.
- **GARCH volatility targeting**: Reacts to past vol, which is already priced in by the time the model adjusts. Consistently produces ~40%+ MDD.
- **FRAMA combinations**: Too conservative in bull markets. Misses the explosive moves that drive long-term compound returns.

### The Monte Carlo Problem

The single biggest challenge facing R&D right now is the Monte Carlo MDD test. Here's the issue in plain terms:

When you randomly shuffle BTC's daily returns (destroying the time sequence), the resulting synthetic price paths almost always produce drawdowns exceeding 40%. This is a fundamental property of BTC's fat-tailed return distribution — the asset can drop 10%+ in a single day, and when you randomly stack several such days together, drawdowns become enormous.

This means that **any strategy whose returns are path-dependent** (i.e., it works because of the *sequence* of returns, not just their magnitude) will fail the MC MDD test. V5.3 passed because its crash defense overlays actively reduce exposure during volatile periods, which works regardless of path ordering. Most other strategies rely on trend persistence, which breaks when you shuffle returns.

The open question for Week 16: can we find a strategy that passes the MC MDD test while also beating V5.3's Sharpe of 2.43? Or is V5.3 the practical ceiling for this asset class?
