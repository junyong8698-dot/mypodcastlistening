How Multi-Agent AI Is Replacing $200/Hour Legal Associates in Document Review

The $10 Billion Document Review Problem When a company gets sued — or sues someone — the first phase is discovery. Both sides must produce relevant documents. In a typical corporate litigation case, this means reviewing 500,000 to 5 million documents to determine which are relevant, privileged, or responsive. At major law firms, this work is done by junior associates and contract attorneys billing $150-400/hour.
