## Reddit Post — r/algotrading

**Title:** I built a 9-member AI board of directors to govern my BTC trading model. Here's what happened.

**Body:**

I've been building a systematic BTC trading system with AI agents (using OpenClaw + Claude). After my R&D agent started generating 22+ experiments per day, I realized I had the classic solo-operator problem: no one to say "are you sure?" before deploying changes.

So I built an AI board of directors. 9 members with distinct personas — 3 progressive (growth-seeking), 3 conservative (risk-focused), 3 moderate (balanced). They meet weekly, review R&D proposals, deliberate with actual arguments, and vote.

Some findings after running this for several weeks:

- The conservative directors caught 3 proposals that had suspiciously short out-of-sample periods
- The board rejected ~60% of proposals, which is actually close to institutional rates
- Deliberation quality improved when I gave directors access to each other's previous arguments
- The whole process takes about 15 minutes and costs ~$2 in API calls
